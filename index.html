About

Growing up in Naharlagun, a small town in Arunachal Pradesh, I didn't have the typical CS kid story. No coding bootcamps at twelve, no startup garage. What I had was curiosity — about how things work, why people make the choices they do, and what happens when you mix the right spices in the wrong order.

That curiosity led me to IISER Bhopal, where I study Computer Science and spend my time thinking about the messy space between algorithms and real human behavior. My research sits at the intersection of human–AI interaction, optimization, and socially relevant computing. I've worked on drone surveillance scheduling, trajectory prediction models, and geospatial inference using LLMs — but what really drives me is the question underneath: how do we build systems that genuinely help people think better?

When I'm not debugging code or reading papers, you'll probably find me cooking a recipe from my never ending list of dishes, writing nostalgic threads of memory on Medium, or disappearing into a travel magazine. I co-founded "Be the Voice" back in school : a small attempt to do something useful. I still carry that impulse.

Research & Projects

01

Multi-Stage Drone Surveillance Scheduling

Designed and evaluated a novel Multi-Stage algorithmic framework for minimizing drone flight time under patrol interval constraints. Tested on real-world university campus maps, achieving high area coverage with significantly lower runtime than traditional approaches.

Optimization · ILP · Simulated Annealing
02

Trajectron: Multi-Agent Trajectory Prediction

Implemented a graph-based deep generative model using Conditional VAEs and dynamic spatiotemporal graphs for predicting pedestrian trajectories. Evaluated on ETH/UCY benchmarks — outperformed baselines like Social GAN on ADE, FDE, and NLL metrics.

Deep Learning · CVAEs · PyTorch
03

Geospatial LLM Inference for Travel

Built the data backbone for an LLM-powered travel decision system — scraping flight data, curating geospatial datasets for Indian destinations, integrating APIs. The kind of infrastructure work that makes "AI-powered" tools actually function.

LLMs · Data Engineering · Geospatial
